Design to Web

What is a responsive site and why is responsiveness important?

A responsive site is designed with the multitude of devices people use to look at website in mind. Different devices mean different screen sizes and a resonsive site keeps that in mind by having images and content adjust to the screen size.

What's mobile first design, and why is it important?

Linking to the fact that websites are being accessed through differnt devices, using a mobile first design means designing the page with the smallest screen in mind and making sure the layout works for small screens, then working up from there.

What are frameworks, and what are their pros and cons?

Frameworks, like Skeleton which I've used here, provide a set range of tools and features to easily create a website and can make building a site really time efficient, plus it's for free and who doesn't like a bargain. A con is that there are limits to what a framework offers and that it can be tempting to simply apply the framework without at least trying to understand the language behind it.

What's a wireframe and why do we use it?

A wireframe is an initial drawing of a website's and its page's layout. It really helps visualise the strcture of the page that's being build and serves as a reference point when designing the page.

Which aspects of my wireframes did I find difficult to implement, and why?

IMAGES! Positioning them, getting them to move the way I would like them to, there is still a bit of learning happening on that front.
